How We Work

1. Request & Idea

You send us your idea, 3D file, or a link to a model. You can also simply describe your concept — we’ll help bring it to life.

2. Analysis & Pricing

We review your design and calculate material, print time, and complexity. You receive a clear and transparent price quote.

3. Approval

Once approved, we start production. We optimize your model if needed for the best print quality.

4. 3D Production

Your design is printed using professional 3D printers. We focus on precision, strength, and quality results.

5. Finishing & Quality Check

Every print is carefully inspected and cleaned if necessary. We ensure everything meets our quality standards.

6. Delivery

Your order is securely packed and shipped or ready for pickup. Fast, safe, and ready to use.

Materials

PLA

PLA is the most commonly used 3D printing material. It is eco-friendly, affordable, and easy to print with. Perfect for decorative models, prototypes, and simple parts.

PETG

PETG is stronger and more flexible than PLA. It is resistant to moisture and chemicals. Ideal for functional parts, mechanical components, and outdoor use.

ABS

ABS is a durable and heat-resistant material. It is widely used for technical and industrial applications. Best for strong parts, enclosures, and high-stress components.

TPU (Flexible)

TPU is a flexible rubber-like material. It is bendable, shock-absorbing, and highly durable. Perfect for phone cases, gaskets, and flexible parts.

Pricing Explained

Our pricing is transparent and based on a few key factors so you always know what you are paying for:


Material (grams)
The amount of filament used in your print. Larger or denser models require more material.


Print Time (hours)
Longer prints take more machine time and energy, which affects the final price.


Model Complexity
Highly detailed or technical designs require slower printing speeds and more precision.


Finishing
Post-processing such as cleaning, sanding, or assembly can add to the total cost.


👉 This way, you only pay for what your project actually needs — nothing more.

Print Quality & Limits

To ensure the best possible results, it’s important to understand the limits and capabilities of our 3D printing process.


Minimum Detail Size
Very small details may not always print clearly. As a general rule, fine details below 0.4mm can lose sharpness depending on the material and design.


Tolerances
Our prints typically have a tolerance of around ±0.2mm. This means small variations in size can occur, which is normal in 3D printing.


What Is Possible
We can produce highly detailed prototypes, functional parts, and custom designs with excellent accuracy and strength.


Limitations
Extremely thin walls, microscopic details, or designs without proper support may not print correctly. In such cases, we may suggest adjustments.


👉 We always aim for the best balance between quality, strength, and design accuracy.
